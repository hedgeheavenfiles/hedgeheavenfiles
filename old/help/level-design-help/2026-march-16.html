My general level design tips:
  • make sure to keep routes spread out from eachother so they each have room to breath and have secrets, but also should reconnect pretty often -make sure to mix up what you're doing from moment to moment alot.
For example, if a part of the level is nothing but holding right and timing your jumps for a while, maybe switch it up with some tricky left and right platforming, or some slopes and loops to roll through or jump off
2
8:30 PM
  • Never forget that slopes and loops can make for engaging gameplay sections too, when used properly.
Downwards and upwards slopes put near eachother forces the player to decide on whether and when to roll, with their choice affecting the speed they gain or lose, Experienced players might jump on the exiting slope of a loop, gaining more speed. So when designing sections like these, always leave room for experimentation. (edited)
1
8:33 PM
Remember to keep top route access methods interesting In your first level, it's fine if the top route is achieved just by a passivity trap or just some platforms you jump up, but as you get further on, try and get into the mind of the player, and put them behind gimmicks, slope jumps, or trickier platforming.

  • Sometimes filler is okay.
Not every part of your level needs to be unique "rooms" With a set path and goal, sometimes it can just be a staircase area like this, with a few badniks, rings, and a semisolid with an item. (edited)
Time 7/20/2025 9:04 PM
  • Design based on your environment.
For a forest or plains setting, you can do smooth hills, lots of semi solids and pillars, plenty of caves, and lots of rock outcroppings to work as blocky platforming. For a city, you can each building form a tall pillar of the level, with roads connecting between them, with the areas through the buildings working as indoor sections, you can even put the bottom route on street level and design it so the semisolids are fire exits you're clambering up! Or maybe your level is a skate park, so you'd want to include more hilly and slopey geometry, with lots and lots of halfpipes and tunnels, and grind rails!
